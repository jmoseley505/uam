Homes for Ukraine: Register for hosting opportunities

Thanks to the generosity of the British people, over 100,000 people from Ukraine have arrived in the UK and are living safely in their sponsors’ homes.

After a few months, many of them need to find a new host while they prepare to live independently, because settling into the UK is taking some guests a little longer than expected.

Guests already living in the UK usually need far less support from their hosts than someone who has just landed in the UK. Many have already found work and settled into their local area, making them more self-sufficient.

What is the difference between a sponsor and a host?

We need to know your nationality so we can work out which elections you’re entitled to vote in. If you cannot provide your nationality, you’ll have to send copies of identity documents through the post.

A host is a person who provides accommodation to people from Ukraine. A host does not have to be a sponsor.
